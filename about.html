About Me

My name is Jesus Lopez Lopez, and I am currently a third-year Environmental Studies student at York University specializing in urban planning and GIS.

Background

My academic and professional interests focus on sustainability, environmental protection, and the use of geospatial technologies to support better decision-making in cities. I enjoy working with data, maps, and real-world planning challenges that connect communities and infrastructure.

Current Work

I am currently working with the Town of Whitby as an Asset Management (GIS) Worker, where I support spatial data organization, infrastructure mapping, and analysis. This experience allows me to apply GIS tools in a real municipal environment and better understand how cities manage assets and plan for long-term sustainability.

Goals

I aim to continue developing my skills in GIS and urban planning, with the goal of contributing to sustainable and resilient communities. I am particularly interested in infrastructure planning, environmental systems, and data-driven urban development.
